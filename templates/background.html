Welcome to LemurNet!

Lemurs are on the edge of extinction. With most of the 100+ species endemic of Madagascar, where they face deforestation and poaching, they constitute one of the most critically endangered groups of mammals.

During my travels to the Red Island, I witnessed how fragile their ecosystem is, but also how many people work hard to preserve the population of primates and their natural habitat. More informally, I also saw how cute lemurs are, and fell in love with them right away!

Combining this with my interest in machine learning and deep learning, I decided to create a tool for the image-based identification of lemur species, with the hope that it can serve an educational or preservation purpose, and raise awareness of the fate of the lemurs of Madagascar. It was also a great opportunity to conduct an end-to-end project and learn a lot from it, and so LemurNet was born!

On this website you can try LemurNet to identify lemur species from pictures, and read a step-by-step guide to implementing your own deep learning solution from scratch to webserving. The neural network runs directly inside the browser, so no data will be collected by this website, but it can take up to a minute or so to load the model. You will also find useful links to learn more about lemurs and some great NGOs that look after them.

Last but not least, I am aware of the shortcomings of LemurNet that a larger, professionally-curated dataset would overcome (see the blog for more details). In the future, I will try to partner with a NGO or a research center to make this tool more accurate and useful to all! The code for this website is open-sourced on GitHub, with Jupyter notebooks available here!

Enjoy LemurNet!
